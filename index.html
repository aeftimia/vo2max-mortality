How to estimate your VO₂ max
  • Smartwatch estimate: Many Garmin, Apple Watch, and Polar devices estimate VO₂ max from heart rate during runs. These are typically within ±5 mL/kg/min.
  • Cooper 12-minute run test: VO₂ max ≈ (distance in meters − 504.9) / 44.73. Run as far as you can in 12 minutes on a flat surface.
  • Rockport 1-mile walk test: Walk 1 mile as fast as possible; use published formula with finish time, heart rate, age, sex, and weight.
  • Clinical exercise test (gold standard): VO₂ max measured directly by a sports medicine lab or cardiologist during maximal exercise.

    Important caveats

    • Clinical cohort bias. Mandsager et al. studied patients referred for exercise treadmill testing — a sicker-than-average population. Their absolute mortality rates are higher than the general population. We use the ratios (hazard ratios) between fitness levels, assuming these transfer to the general population. This may overestimate the benefit of fitness improvement.
    • Correlation vs. causation. Observational HRs do not establish causation. Fitness may partly be a biomarker of underlying health rather than a purely modifiable cause of mortality. Intervention RCTs show real but smaller effects than observational HRs.
    • Independence assumption. Risk factors are applied multiplicatively, assuming independence. Real interactions (e.g., diabetes + CAD) are often synergistic, so combined risk may be overestimated or underestimated.
    • Age-constant HRs. Mandsager HRs are averaged across a wide age range and applied uniformly here. Fitness benefits may vary by age.
    • VO₂ max measurement error. Field estimates (wearables, Cooper test) carry ±5–15 mL/kg/min uncertainty, which can shift category classification.
    • Population generalizability. The Mandsager cohort was predominantly white, middle-aged, Cleveland Clinic patients. Results may differ in other populations.
    • Not medical advice. This tool is for educational purposes only. Consult your physician before making changes to exercise or health decisions.
