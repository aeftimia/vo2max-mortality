    Life expectancy impact

    Computed by integrating the SSA 2022 Period Life Table from your current age to 119, with mortality scaled by the fitness-level HR ratio. This is a simplified model; actual life expectancy depends on many factors.

    Important caveats

    • Clinical cohort bias. The underlying studies included patients referred for exercise treadmill testing (VO₂ max) or UK Biobank volunteers (grip strength) — neither is a random sample. We use only the ratios (hazard ratios) between fitness levels, anchored to general-population life tables. Whether this over- or under-estimates the true benefit depends on whether fitness-related mortality risk is multiplicative (proportional to baseline) or additive. The proportional hazards assumption is standard but imperfect.
    • Correlation vs. causation. Observational HRs do not establish causation. Fitness may partly be a biomarker of underlying health rather than a purely modifiable cause of mortality.
    • Independence assumption. Risk factors are applied multiplicatively, assuming independence. Real interactions are often synergistic, so combined risk may be overestimated or underestimated.
    • Sex-stratified grip strength HR. Unlike VO₂ max (where Kokkinos 2022 found consistent HRs across sex), grip strength HRs differ by sex (Celis-Morales 2018: 1.20 per 5 kg for women vs. 1.16 for men). The calculator accounts for this.
    • Population differences. VO₂ max norms use US data (FRIEND 2022); grip strength norms use Italian data (Lookup 7+). Percentile rankings may differ across populations.
    • Measurement error. Field estimates carry uncertainty that can shift percentile classification.
    • Not medical advice. This tool is for educational purposes only. Consult your physician before making changes to exercise or health decisions.
